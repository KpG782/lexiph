# Barangay Disaster Preparedness and Response Plan
## Barangay San Isidro, Quezon City

**Prepared by:** Barangay Council
**Date:** January 2024

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the disaster preparedness and response plan for Barangay San Isidro in accordance with Republic Act No. 10121 (Philippine Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Act of 2010).

## 2. Barangay Profile

- **Population:** 15,234 residents
- **Number of Households:** 3,456
- **Area:** 2.5 square kilometers
- **Identified Hazards:** Flooding, Fire, Earthquake

## 3. Evacuation Plan

### 3.1 Evacuation Centers
- Primary: Barangay Hall Multi-Purpose Building (Capacity: 500 persons)
- Secondary: San Isidro Elementary School (Capacity: 800 persons)

### 3.2 Evacuation Routes
- Main Route: Bonifacio Street to Barangay Hall
- Alternative Route: Rizal Avenue to Elementary School

### 3.3 Evacuation Procedures
1. Alert system activation via sirens and SMS
2. Barangay tanods assist in orderly evacuation
3. Vulnerable groups (elderly, PWD, children) prioritized
4. Head count at evacuation centers

## 5. Relief Operations

### 5.1 Relief Goods Inventory
- Rice: 100 sacks (25kg each)
- Canned goods: 500 cans
- Bottled water: 200 cases
- Hygiene kits: 150 sets

### 5.2 Distribution Plan
- Distribution point: Barangay Hall covered court
- Priority: Families with children, elderly, PWD
- Documentation: Relief distribution forms

## 6. Medical Response

### 6.1 First Aid Stations
- Location: Barangay Health Center
- Personnel: 2 nurses, 5 trained first aiders
- Equipment: Basic first aid supplies, stretchers

### 6.2 Medical Evacuation
- Coordination with nearest hospital: Quezon City General Hospital
- Ambulance service: Available through barangay hotline

## 7. Communication System

### 7.1 Warning System
- Sirens installed at 3 strategic locations
- SMS blast system (registered residents)
- Barangay radio announcements

### 7.2 Information Dissemination
- Official Facebook page updates
- Barangay bulletin boards
- House-to-house announcements for urgent matters

## 8. Budget Allocation

- Emergency Response Fund: ₱500,000
- Relief Goods Procurement: ₱300,000
- Equipment Maintenance: ₱100,000
- Training & Drills: ₱50,000

**Total Budget:** ₱950,000

## 9. Training and Drills

- Quarterly earthquake drills
- Annual fire safety training
- First aid training for barangay tanods

## 10. Coordination

### 10.1 City Level
- Regular coordination with Quezon City DRRM Office
- Participation in city-wide disaster drills

### 10.2 Community Level
- Purok leaders briefed on disaster protocols
- Community volunteers trained
